Rank-Efficiency Guard Analysis
==============================

Purpose:
Add derived planner metrics for Qwen decision evaluation and test whether they can better identify useful LLM-assisted next-view changes.

New derived metrics:
- weighted_rank
- explore_rank
- distance_rank
- distance_saving_vs_original
- score_drop_ratio_vs_original
- stable_efficiency_ratio

Main candidate policy:
rank_eff_v1_dist05_score09

Rules:
- selected_weighted_rank <= 3
- score_drop_ratio_vs_original <= 0.09
- distance_saving_vs_original >= 0.5
- stable_efficiency_ratio >= 1.0

Offline result:
For apply-v1 changed steps:
- Accepted: [42, 535, 581]
- Rejected: [30, 58, 381]

For strict-v1 changed steps:
- Accepted: [42, 381, 415, 472]
- Rejected: [30]

Interpretation:
The policy rejects suspicious or weak changes in apply-v1:
- step 58 has no distance saving and lower exploration efficiency.
- step 381 has very small distance saving and almost no efficiency gain.

The policy preserves stronger distance-rescue changes:
- step 42
- step 535
- step 581

Conclusion:
Rank-efficiency guard is more targeted than simply tightening the original guard.
It supports a next-stage transition from LLM-assisted planner to LLM-primary planner with richer planner-derived metrics and fallback safety.
